18-549: Spring 2013

Lance Christian, Mike Gardner, Arjuna Hayes, Lawrence Tsang

In the Hitchhikers Guide to the Galaxy, there was a Nutri-matic robot that could concoct any drink your heart desired.
In Star Trek, they had a food synthesizer which would turn food-menu-cards into full meals.
The concept of autonomous consumable creation is common among sci-fi writers, but we intend to turn that dream into a reality.

What does it do?

It makes you cereal. It pours you soda. It makes you a smoothie. It makes you a frozen daiquiri. It makes you hot coffee after you realize the tenth daiquiri might have been a bad idea. And it can do it all at once.

Basically, The Beverage (and More) Dispenser has a set of removable, temperature controlled containers which can be filled with any combination of liquid or solid consumables. It can then be set to dispense them in precisely measured quantities into a blender cup. This allows the user to set up a list of items the machine can produce, making it easy to make a consumable with the press of a button. Put cold milk, cereal, coffee powder, and hot water into it and it can make breakfast. Put ice, diced fruits, sugar, and milk in it to make a smoothie machine. There are so many possibilities.

It can even be set to make drinks on a timer. Getting ready to leave from work? Have a chilled drink waiting for you when you get home. Need your coffee in the morning? It can have it hot by the time you drag yourself out of bed.

Why make it?

How awesome would it be to have this at home, making your breakfast as your alarm goes off? How awesome would this be to have at a party, mixing drinks for your guests? How awesome would this be for preparing a drink for you before you get home from work?

We want one. We think a lot of people would want one. It's a commercially viable, fiscally reasonable, and totally sweet project. What more could you want?

Risks and Mitigations

Risks Mitigations
Temperature control doesn't work (1) Remove initial plan to use arbitrary temperature control, and go with 1 hot and 1 cold chamber, (2) try 1 hot OR 1 cold chamber, or (3) look for alternative methods of temperature control (like heating coils)
Temperature control works Of course it does
Powering all of the devices: motors, solenoid valves, and TEC have specific power needs We are planning to use batteries for the motors and solenoid valves, and a portable current supply for the TEC
No experience with mechanical elements We have access to MechE’s who will be crucial resources when using the solenoid valves and motors
